# Mastery and balance targets

The controls do not change with difficulty. Higher mastery changes readable combinations: escort count, route pressure, visible modifiers, optional objectives, and Sheriff plan. It never introduces hidden actions or random instant failure.

## Rotating modifiers

- Armored Escort: stun three guards instead of two before robbery.
- Scarce Quivers: each outlaw starts with one fewer arrow.
- Double Tithe: carts carry 150 coin and the village target rises to 750.
- Watchful Sheriff: one additional escort guard is visible from mission start.

Every room receives two deterministic modifiers from its mission seed. Both names and effects appear under the objective.

## Adaptive Sheriff plans

- Patrol: opening shipment and smaller parties.
- Pursuit: later shipments combine route pressure with existing Wanted behavior.
- Reinforcement: four-player bands or late shipments receive the largest visible escort combination.

## Character and party targets

| Target | Robin | Marian |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scout | Signals route and covers the band | Fastest landmark traversal |
| Ambush | Highest reliable multi-stun output | Veil creates safe positioning and heat control |
| Pursuit | Protects the loot carrier | Breaks pursuit and reaches rescue angles |
| Support | Revive, ping, and transfer available | Revive, ping, and transfer available |

- Solo is a practice mode, not a verified four-player balance target.
- Two players must be completable with either Robin/Marian or two of one role.
- Four players add guards and coordination load rather than inflating guard health.
- Expected success after tutorial completion: 55–70% for new bands, 80–90% for practiced bands.

Tutorial completion is recorded after the first band reaches the Robbery phase. Mission events already provide phase, route, support, capture, vote, and result telemetry for the operations pipeline.
